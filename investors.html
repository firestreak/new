Overview

Platelet BioGenesis is a pre-clinical stage company that is developing a microfluidic bioreactor to trigger physiological platelet production at clinical scale from human induced pluripotent stem cells (hiPSCs).

Platelets are the 'band-aids' of the bloodstream, responsible for clot formation and blood vessel repair. Platelet units are derived exclusively from human volunteer donors, and represent a critical first-line therapy following trauma, radiation exposure, cancer-treatment, transplant, and surgery. Low platelet count (thrombocytopenia) is also a significant consequence of a radiation accident (eg. Fukushima, Chernobyl) or deliberate nuclear attack. Platelet availability was recently identified by The United States Allergy and Infectious Diseases Radiation Countermeasures Program as a major unmet need following a radiation accident or attack.

More than 2 million Americans receive life-saving platelet transfusions annually. Demand for platelets in the United States exceeds supply by over 533K platelet units/year and the available product is risky. Replacing the current donor-based system is a $1.7 billion/year opportunity domestically, and considerably larger globally.

Our primary competition is donor-derived platelet units. Unlike red blood cells, platelets are especially sensitive to irreversible activation/aggregation below 22 ⁰C, and their short shelf life (5 days) is due to the associated risk of bacterial/viral contamination during storage. The need to give multiple transfusions to effected individuals is therefore severely constrained by the limited availability of platelet units when a large population size has been exposed, and there are currently no licensed therapeutics, and insufficient platelet supply to meet projected demands in the Strategic National Stockpile.

Co-Founder
